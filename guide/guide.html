Info over tomaten

Tomatoes need full sunlight, at least 6-8 hours per day.

Carrot Care Guide

Planting

Plant carrot seeds 1/4 inch deep, 1-2 inches apart, in loose, well-drained soil.

Watering

Carrots need moderate watering. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.

Sunlight

Carrots grow best in full sunlight (6-8 hours a day).

Soil

Carrots prefer loose, sandy soil with good drainage and a pH of 6.0 to 6.8.

Harvesting

Harvest carrots 2-4 months after planting when they are firm and bright orange. Pull gently to avoid breaking the root.

Lettuce Care Guide

Planting

Plant lettuce seeds 1/8 inch deep in cool, moist soil.

Watering

Lettuce requires frequent, light watering to keep the soil moist but not soggy.

Sunlight

Lettuce grows best in full to partial sunlight, about 4-6 hours a day.

Soil

Lettuce prefers loose, well-drained soil, with a slightly acidic pH of 6.0 to 7.0.

Harvesting

Harvest lettuce when leaves are large enough to eat, typically 30-60 days after planting.

Spinach Care Guide

Planting

Plant spinach seeds 1/2 inch deep, 2-4 inches apart, in well-drained, cool soil.

Watering

Spinach needs regular watering to keep the soil consistently moist but not soggy.

Sunlight

Spinach prefers partial to full sunlight. It thrives in cool weather.

Soil

Spinach grows best in rich, well-drained soil with a slightly acidic pH (6.0 to 7.0).

Harvesting

Harvest spinach when leaves are large enough to eat. Typically 40-50 days after planting.
